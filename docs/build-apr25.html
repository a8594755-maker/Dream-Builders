Build Station Layout and Organization

The build area will be organized into 8 build stations, each capable of producing 5 beds. Stations are designed for optimal workflow, safety, and volunteer experience.

Station 1: Cutting & Preparation

Capacity: 5 beds
Volunteers: 4-5
Tools: Saws, measuring tapes, clamps, safety equipment
Process: Measure and cut all lumber pieces according to bed specifications

Station 2: Frame Assembly

Capacity: 5 beds
Volunteers: 4-5
Tools: Drills, screws, squares, clamps
Process: Assemble bed frame components and secure connections

Station 3: Sanding & Finishing

Capacity: 5 beds
Volunteers: 3-4
Tools: Sandpaper, routers, safety equipment
Process: Sand all surfaces smooth and apply finish as needed

Station 4: Headboard Construction

Capacity: 5 beds
Volunteers: 3-4
Tools: Drills, saws, measuring equipment
Process: Construct and finish headboard components

Station 5: Assembly Integration

Capacity: 5 beds
Volunteers: 4-5
Tools: Drills, wrenches, assembly hardware
Process: Integrate all components into complete bed assembly

Station 6: Quality Control

Capacity: 5 beds
Volunteers: 2-3
Tools: Measuring equipment, inspection checklists
Process: Inspect completed beds for quality and safety standards

Station 7: Bedding Preparation

Capacity: 5 bed sets
Volunteers: 3-4
Tools: None (bedding only)
Process: Prepare and organize bedding sets for each bed

Station 8: Final Assembly & Packaging

Capacity: 5 complete beds
Volunteers: 3-4
Tools: Basic assembly tools
Process: Final assembly, bedding placement, and delivery preparation

Safety Plan and Procedures

Safety Measures and Protocols

üë§ Personal Safety

  • Mandatory safety glasses at all times in build area
  • Work gloves required for handling lumber and materials
  • Ear protection required when using power tools
  • Appropriate footwear (closed-toe, sturdy shoes)
  • No loose clothing or jewelry that could catch in tools

üîß Tool Safety

  • Tool safety briefing before use
  • Experienced volunteer supervision for power tools
  • Proper tool storage and maintenance
  • Regular tool inspection for damage or wear
  • Clear work areas and proper tool handling

üèóÔ∏è Site Safety

  • Clear walkways and organized work stations
  • Proper ventilation and dust control
  • Fire safety equipment readily available
  • Emergency exit routes clearly marked
  • Weather contingency plans for outdoor activities

üöë Emergency Procedures

  • First aid stations at multiple locations
  • Emergency contact information posted
  • Trained first aid volunteers on site
  • Emergency evacuation plan and rally points
  • Local emergency services notified of event
