Discussion Notes

Funding Sources

The overwhelming majority of funding for SHP–NRV comes from local sources, including individual donors (including recurring monthly donors), local organizations and companies, and churches (example: a church raising $15,000 in a single donation). National funding is occasional and occurs when a large donor contributes to SHP National, which then distributes funds across approximately 350 chapters. National sponsors (e.g., Lowe's) sometimes provide funds that are divided among chapters.

Build Events & Website Listings

Not all builds are listed publicly on the SHP website. Builds may be closed (invite-only) or open to the public. Public builds allow volunteers to register online. Closed builds (e.g., football team, fraternity, or police department builds) are not listed to prevent excessive public sign-ups. After closed builds, photos and updates are shared on social media instead.

Bed Build & Delivery Process

Beds are built in components (headboards, footboards, side rails, slats) and assembled using lag bolts and screws. Beds are stained with a vinegar-and-steel-wool solution for cost efficiency and pest resistance. Each bed includes a new mattress, bedding ("bed-in-a-bag" preferred), a pillow, and sometimes a stuffed animal. Bedding is sourced individually in bulk from retailers such as Amazon, Walmart, and Kohl's.

Student Involvement & April Builds

April 10: Observation/run-through build opportunity. April 25: Primary build event (target: 40 beds). Students will assist with planning, preparation, and execution rather than redesigning the existing SHP process. Emphasis on reverse planning, milestones, and "lines of effort" (manpower, materials, location, logistics).

Expectations

Clear, timely, and precise communication. Defined responsibilities and follow-through. Candid feedback if project constraints conflict with academic requirements. One primary student point of contact for communication.
