Lessons Learned

Reflections on our project journey and key takeaways for future success

After Project Kickoff

February 2026

What Went Well

  • Strong team engagement and enthusiasm from the start
  • Clear project scope and goals established early
  • Good initial stakeholder identification and communication
  • Successful partnership establishment with SHP–NRV

What Didn't Go Well

  • Initial timeline was overly ambitious given team availability
  • Unclear decision-making processes in early meetings
  • Insufficient time allocated for volunteer recruitment planning
  • Limited understanding of build day requirements initially

What We Changed

  • Revised project timeline with more realistic milestones
  • Established clear roles and single point of contact (Holden)
  • Created detailed communication plan and meeting cadence
  • Scheduled observation build to better understand requirements

PM Takeaway

Reverse Planning Critical: Starting from the April 25 main build date and working backwards helped identify critical path activities and realistic timelines. Clear role definition and communication protocols prevent confusion and ensure accountability.

After Outreach Planning

March 2026

What Went Well

  • Comprehensive stakeholder mapping completed
  • Multiple outreach channels identified and prioritized
  • Strong messaging framework developed around mission
  • Good integration with SHP–NRV existing networks

What Didn't Go Well

  • Underestimated time needed for donor follow-up
  • Initial fundraising goals not aligned with community capacity
  • Competing community events affected outreach timing
  • Volunteer recruitment messaging needed refinement

What We Changed

  • Adjusted fundraising targets based on community feedback
  • Created tiered sponsorship approach for different donor levels
  • Developed more specific volunteer role descriptions
  • Implemented systematic follow-up schedule for donor contacts

PM Takeaway

Stakeholder Management: Understanding community capacity and timing constraints is essential for effective outreach. Tiered approaches to engagement (volunteers, donors, sponsors) allow for more targeted and successful communication strategies.

After April 10 Observation Build

April 2026

What Went Well

  • Excellent hands-on learning experience for team members
  • Clear understanding of build process and requirements
  • Strong relationship building with experienced volunteers
  • Identification of critical tools and material needs

What Didn't Go Well

  • Underestimated space requirements for build activities
  • Tool organization and workflow efficiency needs improvement
  • Weather contingency planning was insufficient
  • Volunteer skill assessment could have been more thorough

What We Changed

  • Revised main build layout and station organization
  • Created detailed tool inventory and preparation checklist
  • Developed comprehensive weather backup plans
  • Implemented volunteer skill assessment and role assignment system

PM Takeaway

Operational Planning: Direct observation and participation revealed critical operational details that planning alone could not identify. Physical space, workflow efficiency, and weather contingency planning are essential for successful execution.

After April 25 Main Build

April 2026

What Went Well

  • Successfully met target of 40 beds built
  • Excellent volunteer turnout and engagement
  • Strong community support and visibility
  • Effective coordination with SHP–NRV leadership

What Didn't Go Well

  • Material shortages required last-minute adjustments
  • Some volunteer roles were over/under-staffed
  • Timeline extended due to quality control needs
  • Documentation during event could have been better

What We Changed

  • Implemented just-in-time material delivery system
  • Created flexible volunteer role assignment protocols
  • Built buffer time into future build schedules
  • Established dedicated documentation and media team

PM Takeaway

Execution Excellence: Success requires both detailed planning and adaptive execution. Flexibility in volunteer management, material logistics, and timeline management is essential when dealing with complex, time-sensitive community projects.

Final Project Wrap-up

May 2026

What Went Well

  • Achieved all primary project goals and objectives
  • Strong community impact and awareness generated
  • Sustainable partnership model established
  • Comprehensive documentation and lessons captured

What Didn't Go Well

  • Initial timeline pressure caused early stress
  • Some donor relationships could have been nurtured better
  • Media coverage was less than anticipated
  • Succession planning for ongoing efforts needs work

What We Changed

  • Developed more realistic project planning framework
  • Created donor stewardship and recognition program
  • Established media outreach and storytelling protocols
  • Built transition plan for ongoing community partnership

PM Takeaway

Project Sustainability: Successful project completion requires attention to both immediate goals and long-term impact. Building sustainable processes, nurturing stakeholder relationships, and planning for continuity ensures that project benefits extend beyond the initial timeline.
